Program Overview

About BBFF High School Chapters

BBFF High School Chapters are city-based, student-led organizations that operate under the parent 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Each chapter raises awareness about pediatric cancer, improves visibility for the BBFF mission, and organizes fundraising events.

This program is designed for student leaders who want to make a meaningful difference in the fight against pediatric cancer. Each chapter is encouraged to adapt guidelines to fit their local community, school culture, and team strengths.

Organizational Structure

Parent Organization: Brenden Butler Family Foundation (501(c)(3))
Oversight Body: BBFF High School Program Board
Local Units: City-based chapters (e.g., Frisco Chapter, Dallas Chapter)

Each city chapter operates semi-independently but reports to the High School Program Board. BBFF provides mission direction, branded resources, and tracks total program impact and funds raised.

Chapter Goals

  • Raise Awareness about pediatric cancer in your local community through campaigns and events
  • Increase Visibility and support for the Brenden Butler Family Foundation's mission
  • Organize Events & Fundraisers that directly benefit families fighting childhood cancer
  • Build Community with student-led, flexible, accountable, and scalable leadership
Who should get involved?
Students interested in starting or joining a chapter, faculty advisors, parents, and community members passionate about supporting families battling pediatric cancer.
Chapter Leadership

Officer positions

Every chapter has four required leadership roles. Additional roles may be created as the chapter grows.

President

Oversees all chapter operations and meetings. Serves as primary contact with the Board and represents the chapter at BBFF events.

Vice President

Supports the President and assumes duties in their absence. Coordinates between committees and general members, helps plan events.

Treasurer

Manages chapter finances and fundraising records. Tracks all donations and expenditures, provides monthly financial summaries.

Outreach Director

Manages social media, recruitment, and community partnerships. Coordinates with schools and local businesses, drives membership growth.

Additional roles available: Event Coordinator, Secretary, Social Media Manager, Volunteer Coordinator

Who can join?

Any high school student passionate about making a difference for pediatric cancer families. No prior experience needed — just a willingness to lead, learn, and serve.

Members attend regular meetings, volunteer at events, may serve on event-specific committees, and are eligible for leadership positions through the succession process.

Standards

Code of Conduct

Represent the BBFF mission with integrity and respect
Treat all members, families, and partners with kindness and professionalism
Maintain transparency in all financial matters
Avoid conflicts of interest in leadership decisions
Protect the privacy of families and individuals served by BBFF
Follow all school policies and local regulations for student organizations
Enforcement: 1st offense → Written warning · 2nd offense → Temporary suspension · 3rd offense → Removal from chapter
Questions

Frequently asked

Any high school student (grades 9–12) can join a BBFF chapter. There are no minimum GPA or prior experience requirements. You just need a passion for helping others.

Officers meet at least once per month. General member meetings are recommended bi-weekly or monthly. Pre-event meetings are optional but earn extra service hours. Most members contribute 2–5 hours per month.

Yes! All chapter activities count toward community service hours. We provide verified hour reports that can be submitted to your school, NHS, or college applications.

Each chapter can choose their platform. We recommend the Remind app for ease of use, but GroupMe, Discord, and Google Classroom are also common options.

Reports are due by the 15th of the month following each quarter: Q1 (Jan–Mar) due April 15, Q2 (Apr–Jun) due July 15, Q3 (Jul–Sep) due October 15, Q4 (Oct–Dec) due January 15. Reports include membership count, funds raised, events held, and community impact summary.

Succession planning begins 2 months before anticipated changes. Current officers identify and interview candidates based on commitment, impact, and mission alignment. A 2-week overlap period ensures smooth transition of records, passwords, and resources.
